Community Service Information

The Community Service Requirement
 

Each student at Lexington High School is required to complete a minimum of 40 hours of community service during their high school career (transfer students must perform 10 hours per year of attendance at LHS) in order to graduate. Students must complete 20 hours of community service to be eligible for open campus. Juniors entering the year in September must have 20 hours of community service documented and seniors entering the year in September must have 30 hours documented. Students may begin accruing hours beginning the summer prior to ninth grade. The yearly community service requirement may be satisfied by participation in either a single activity or a combination of approved activities.

Definition of Community Service
Community Service is defined as an act or activity performed by an individual without compensation or academic credit, whose time and effort will benefit/aid a person or group of people and is without direct religious or political involvement.
A community service activity must conform to the following guidelines:

  • It must be performed without compensation to the student. Examples: Court-ordered community service will not be accepted. Paid work will not be accepted. Work receiving academic credit will not be accepted.
  • It must benefit at least one person other than the student or the student's relative(s).
  • It must not be associated with the practice, promotion or ceremonies of any religion. It must not be associated with religious services, education, proselytizing, or fund-raising. A non-religious activity performed through a religious community, but which does not benefit the religious community directly, may still be considered.
  • Political campaigning or work benefiting political organizations or causes directly cannot be accepted.

Process for Earning Community Service Credit

Credit must be earned according to the following guidelines.

  • The service must meet the prior definition.
  • The service may not be used to earn credit for more than one program or organization.  For example, Peer Tutors may earn community service credit toward LHS or NHS requirements, but not both; students who volunteer in school offices may receive either personnel service credit or community service credit, but not both.
  • The service can be performed for an approved organization or if in doubt, the service must be approved beforehand by submitting a completed Pre-approval form prior to the service. 
  • The service and hours must be verifiable with the service contact person.
  • The service must be reported within 90 days of completion of posted submission deadline.
  • Completed and legible Community Service Forms must be placed in the community Service bin in the Library Media Center Rm. #151.  Incomplete or late forms may delay the processing of your hours.

Approved Service Opportunities
The Community Service Secretary maintains a list of Approved Service Opportunities.   The list is also posted on the Community Service bulletin board in the Guidance Department of the Arts and Humanities building. Please refer to this list if you are having difficulty finding an opportunity to serve or if you are unsure if an organization has been previously approved.
